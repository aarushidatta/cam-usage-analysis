# CAM Usage Analysis Across Age Groups

## Project Overview
This study presents a statistical and data-driven analysis of the adoption of Complementary and Alternative Medicine (CAM) using primary survey data collected from 255 respondents. The objective is to understand the demographic, medical, and behavioral factors influencing CAM usage using statistical techniques.

The study integrates descriptive analysis with inferential methods such as logistic regression and chi-square testing to provide a comprehensive understanding of CAM adoption behavior.

---

## Objectives
- To analyze CAM usage patterns across demographic groups  
- To identify key factors influencing CAM adoption  
- To examine associations between medical conditions and CAM usage  
- To build a predictive model for CAM usage behavior  
- To understand perceptions and behavioral drivers of CAM adoption  

---

## Methodology

### 1. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
- Studied distributions and relationships among variables  
- Identified patterns across age, gender, and health conditions  
- Performed visual analysis of usage trends  

### 2. Data Preprocessing
- Handled class imbalance using SMOTE  
- Encoded categorical variables for analysis  

### 3. Statistical & Inferential Analysis
- Chi-Square Test of Independence  
- Logistic Regression modeling  
- Measures of association  
- Hypothesis testing  

---

## Key Findings
- CAM adoption rate in the dataset is 22.4%, indicating limited but structured usage patterns  
- Age and gender are significant predictors of CAM adoption, with older individuals and females showing higher likelihood of usage  
- CAM is primarily used as a complementary therapy alongside conventional medicine rather than as a replacement  
- Individuals using prescription medicines are more likely to also adopt CAM, indicating an integrative healthcare approach  
- Chronic diseases (e.g., diabetes, arthritis, cholesterol) are strongly associated with regular CAM usage, while acute conditions lead to occasional use  
- Homoeopathy, Ayurveda, and Yoga are the most commonly used modalities, often used in combination  
- Perception of CAM is generally positive, with safety rated highest, but trust in practitioners comparatively lower  
- Adoption is largely influenced by informal sources such as family and friends rather than formal medical guidance, highlighting gaps in structured healthcare communication
